Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies
by McKinsey & Company Inc., Tim Koller,
Marc Goedhart, David Wessels

Publication date: 1990

"Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies" is a practical guide to the art
and science of corporate valuation. Written by McKinsey & Company, this book provides
valuable insights into the different valuation methods used in the corporate world, including
discounted cash flow analysis, market multiples, and precedent transactions.

The book offers step-by-step guidance on how to apply these valuation techniques in
practice, covering topics such as capital structure, risk management, and valuation in
mergers and acquisitions. With its clear explanations and numerous real-world examples,
"Valuation" is an essential resource for professionals in investment banking, private equity,
and corporate finance, as well as for students and academics in business schools.

First published in 1990, this updated and expanded edition of "Valuation" remains a widely-used
and highly-regarded reference book in the field of corporate finance. Whether you're a seasoned
finance professional or just starting out, "Valuation" is a must-read for anyone looking to better
understand how companies are valued in today's dynamic business environment.
