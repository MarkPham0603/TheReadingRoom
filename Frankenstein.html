Frankenstein (1818)

"Frankenstein" by Mary Shelley

Publication date: January 1st 1818

"Frankenstein" is a classic Gothic novel that delves into the darker aspects of human nature.
It follows the story of Victor Frankenstein, a young scientist who becomes obsessed with
creating life from death. After successfully bringing a being to life, he is horrified by the
monster he has created.

The novel explores themes of ambition, guilt, and the consequences of playing God. Mary
Shelley's evocative writing and eerie imagery create a haunting atmosphere that lingers long
after the book is finished.

First published in 1818, "Frankenstein" remains a timeless classic that continues to captivate
readers with its chilling tale of human ambition and its terrifying consequences.
